Chapter 01 · The Problem

Healthcare workers deserve better than a hotel room.

BC needs 11,000 more healthcare workers by 2030. A shortage of quality workforce housing is making a hard problem worse.

🏨
$195/night hotel rooms
Health authorities paying premium hotel rates for short-term accommodation. No stability, no community, no kitchen.
📍
Remote locations, zero supply
Construction crews and healthcare workers sent to Prince George, Fort St. John, Terrace — cities with no real housing infrastructure for workers.
📊
Fragmented billing chaos
Multiple invoices, multiple providers, zero visibility. Finance teams spending hours reconciling per diems that should be a single line item.
💔
Retention is suffering
Healthcare workers leave communities because they can't find stable housing. The workplace isn't the problem — the infrastructure around it is.

Chapter 06 · Healthcare Housing Hubs

More than housing.
Living infrastructure.

A Healthcare Housing Hub isn't a building — it's a community. Designed from the ground up to support the health, performance, and wellbeing of the people who keep BC's healthcare system running.

🌱
Greenhouses
On-site food production integrated into every hub. Fresh produce, wellness garden, connection to the natural world. Health starts with what you eat.
🌿
Green Rooftop Patios
Fully wrapped green rooftops. Community gathering space, outdoor wellness areas, gardens. The natural environment isn't background — it's built into the structure.
🏥
Practitioner Spaces
Office leases for adjacent healthcare services within the hub — physiotherapy, mental health, primary care. The community we're building supports the work they're doing.
🎉
Community Programming
Events, fitness, outdoor adventures through Elias Adventures, cultural programming. When the people living here feel connected, they stay — and that's better for the communities they serve.

What a Hub delivers

When a new hospital opens, ERS is already there. The construction crew lived in our housing while they built it. Now the permanent staff move in. The hub grows with the community.

This is why we're not just a housing company. We're building the infrastructure that makes healthcare communities sustainable — for the people who work in them, and the communities they serve.
